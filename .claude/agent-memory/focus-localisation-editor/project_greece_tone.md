Greece focus descriptions use simple present tense throughout (e.g., "Greece positions itself", "Greece establishes", "coordinates"). The capstone/summary focus `GRE_se_european_pillar_desc` initially used past perfect ("has cemented") which was inconsistent and corrected to simple present.

Focus names follow standard Title Case. Compound focus names (two nouns joined conceptually) should use a hyphen: "Italy-Mediterranean Axis" not "Italy Mediterranean Axis".

Descriptions are encyclopedic, 1–3 sentences, grounded in real-world institutions and data (bilateral trade figures, real summit names like the 2003 Thessaloniki Summit, real infrastructure like Alexandroupolis FSRU, Revithoussa terminal, IMEC corridor). Do not invent or simplify these references.

**Why:** Observed during review of the Regional Diplomacy Branch (8 new focuses, April 2026).
**How to apply:** When editing GRE localisation, maintain simple present tense in all desc strings and verify compound focus names carry hyphens where two nouns are joined.
